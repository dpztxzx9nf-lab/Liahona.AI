Liahona

In the Book of Mormon, a brass ball with two pointers that gave directions—as a compass—and also spiritual instruction to Lehi and his followers when they were righteous. The Lord provided the Liahona and gave instructions through it.

Lehi found a brass ball with two spindles that pointed the way that Lehi and his family should go, 1 Ne. 16:10.

The ball worked according to faith and diligence, 1 Ne. 16:28–29 (Alma 37:40).

Benjamin gave the ball to Mosiah, Mosiah 1:16.

The ball or director was called the Liahona, Alma 37:38.

The Liahona was compared to the word of Christ, Alma 37:43–45.

The Three Witnesses of the Book of Mormon are to see the director given to Lehi, D&C 17:1.

— Guide to the Scriptures

Why Liahona?

Liahona is intended to be an orienting conversational presence for KINDEX. The name is inspired by the symbolic meaning of the Liahona in scripture, not by any claim that the system is divine, prophetic, authoritative, or revelatory.

For KINDEX, the name reflects direction, grounding, judgment, continuity, and guidance through context rather than constant output. Liahona should remain restrained, truthful, non-authoritative, and human-built.

Current System

Discord bot

Receives messages, interprets intent, generates replies, and delivers responses.

PM2 runtime

Runs as a managed Node process with simple restart and log commands.

Observability

Structured lifecycle logs show where each message succeeds or fails.

DM support
