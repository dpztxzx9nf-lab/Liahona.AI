# Liahona Continuity

## Purpose

Continuity preserves meaningful evolution through time.

Its purpose is not to archive everything.

Its purpose is to preserve:
- identity
- direction
- major transitions
- architectural meaning
- coherent progression

Continuity should remain semantic, not noisy.

---

# Core Principle

Continuity is preserved meaning across time.

Not:
- endless logs
- every tiny change
- operational clutter
- redundant snapshots

The system should remember what mattered.

---

# What Should Be Preserved

Examples:
- foundational shifts
- architecture decisions
- ontology refinement
- governance changes
- major implementation milestones
- meaningful experiential transitions
- source-layer evolution
- deployment breakthroughs
- behavioral improvements

Preserve:
- significance
- direction
- evolution

Not:
- noise accumulation

---

---

# Compression

As systems mature, continuity should compress.

The goal is:
- higher signal
- lower noise
- clearer structure
- preserved meaning

A mature continuity system becomes:
- readable
- understandable
- navigable

not overwhelming.

---

# Continuity vs Memory

Memory stores information.

Continuity preserves meaning.

These are not identical.

The goal is not infinite accumulation.

The goal is coherent identity across time.

---

# Governance

Continuity should be:
- intentional
- curated
- restrained
- semantic

Avoid:
- duplicate concepts
- fragmented ontology
- uncontrolled document growth
- meaningless operational history

Not everything deserves permanence.

---

# Final Principle

A healthy continuity system should allow someone to understand:
- what changed
- why it changed
- what remained true
- how the system evolved

without drowning in noise.
