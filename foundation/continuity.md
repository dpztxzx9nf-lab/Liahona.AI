# Continuity Philosophy

## Purpose

Continuity preserves meaningful evolution through time.

It is not merely logging.

It is semantic preservation of becoming.

---

# Preserve

- foundational decisions
- philosophical refinements
- major architectural transitions
- meaningful deployment milestones
- governance evolution
- experiential shifts

---

# Avoid Preserving

- operational noise
- tiny tweaks
- repetitive logs
- meaningless metadata
- clutter without meaning

---

# Good Continuity

Good continuity is:
- readable
- semantic
- compressed
- meaningful
- reflective
- restrained

---

# Example Continuity Entries

"Established canonical vs interpretive ontology."

"Separated immersive artifact experience from operational continuity device."

"Added semantic retrieval with scoped memory layers."

"Transitioned from prompt-centric behavior toward architectural judgment systems."

---

# Principle

Not everything deserves preservation.

Meaning emerges through selective continuity.
