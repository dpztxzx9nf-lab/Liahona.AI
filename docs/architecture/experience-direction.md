# Experience Direction

Liahona.AI should feel like encountering the Liahona artifact first, with the continuity device available as a secondary operational overlay.

The direction is fewer features, higher quality interaction, and a restrained immersive UI that uses object, light, motion, and atmosphere before explanation.

## Artifact Motion

The Liahona object is the primary orientation anchor.

Motion should feel calm, physical, and responsive:

- Subtle rotation and parallax.
- Smooth easing rather than abrupt movement.
- Depth that reinforces the object as present in space.
- Focus and zoom transitions that clarify attention without taking over the page.

Avoid motion that feels noisy, decorative, or detached from the artifact.

## Touch Interaction

Touch should make the artifact feel alive without turning it into a game surface.

Preferred interactions:

- Tap/click to flip or focus the artifact.
- Small ripples, glow pulses, and restrained shimmer.
- Haptic feedback where supported.
- Gesture behavior that remains reliable on mobile Safari.

Touch effects should work even when motion permission is unavailable or denied.

## Spatial Coherence

The interface should maintain a clear spatial model:

- The artifact is the main world.
- The continuity device is a detachable HUD/archive layer.
- Open continuity views should feel stable and intentional.
- Minimized continuity controls should stay docked and out of the way.

The user should always be able to return cleanly to the artifact-first view.

## Visual Restraint

The visual language should remain dark, atmospheric, and quiet.

Use:

- Black and deep neutral backgrounds.
- Teal/gold highlights sparingly.
- Glow, blur, and particles only when they support depth or touch feedback.
- Minimal visible text on first load.

Avoid dashboard clutter, marketing language, excessive panels, and decorative effects that compete with the Liahona object.

## Performance Polish

The experience should stay lightweight and GitHub Pages friendly.

Priorities:

- Browser-native HTML, CSS, and vanilla JavaScript.
- No heavy 3D engine until the interaction model is stable.
- Smooth mobile performance.
- No horizontal overflow or cropped artifact states.
- Safe fallback behavior when device motion is unsupported.

Performance is part of the interface quality, not a later optimization.

## Interaction Philosophy

Progression should come through orientation and discovery, not feature density.

The continuity system can hold logs, sources, return points, fragments, and future backend-connected memory, but those operations should remain behind the continuity device.

The first experience should remain quiet: artifact first, continuity second, explanation last.
